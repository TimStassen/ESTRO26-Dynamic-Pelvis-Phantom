ESTRO26: Dynamic Tissue-Equivalent Pelvis Phantom

Tim Stassen, Luana de Freitas Nascimento, Frank Verhaegen, and Gabriel Paiva Fonseca

Objective

Online Adaptive Radiotherapy (oART) requires real-time adjustments to account for anatomical variations. However, verifying dose delivery during rapid physiological changes—such as bladder filling—remains a significant challenge.

  • Gap: Traditional phantoms lack realistic internal anatomy.
  • Limitation: Current tools have a limited ability to simulate continuous organ motion.

Proposed Solution

We developed a realistic 3D-printed pelvic phantom characterized by:

  • High-fidelity anatomical structures (muscle, fat, bone).
  • A dynamic bladder system featuring controlled volumetric filling and emptying.

Anthropomorphic Phantom Development

The development follows a four-step optimization process. First, candidate materials are selected and printed as samples. These are scanned using Dual Energy CT (DECT) to determine Material Properties: Relative Electron Density (RED) and Effective Atomic Number (Zeff).

By varying 3D printing parameters (infill/pattern), we match the RED values to reference standards (ICRU44). Once optimized, these settings are applied to complex anatomical geometries to produce the final verified phantom.

Dynamic Bladder System

The phantom (187 × 287 × 70 mm3) incorporates nine distinct anatomical structures. Using our in-house AMIGOpy software, we optimized parameters to match realistic CT Hounsfield Units.

System Components:
  • Hollow bladder cavity with integrated dual-access channels.
  • Internal balloon coupled to a precision peristaltic pump.
  • Surrounding water-equivalent gel to mimic soft-tissue displacement.

Results & Validation

Verification was performed using CT (120 kV) and CBCT (125 kV) at filling volumes of 10 ml, 40 ml, and 70 ml.

Tissue Type CT (HU) CBCT (HU)
Skeletal Muscle 157 161
Adipose Tissue 24.5 19.0
Cortical Bone 596 761

Bladder Volume Accuracy:

  • 10 ml: 10–13% deviation
  • 40 ml: 4–5% deviation
  • 70 ml: 1–2% deviation

Absolute volume differences were negligible, ranging from only 0.9 to 1.9 ml.

Conclusion

This study successfully demonstrates a controllable, dynamic phantom. By enabling accurate simulation of physiological changes, this tool improves the validation of online adaptive workflows and allows for more complex clinical scenario testing.
