ESTRO26: Dynamic Tissue-Equivalent Pelvis Phantom

Tim Stassen, Luana de Freitas Nascimento, Frank Verhaegen and Gabriel Paiva Fonseca

Main Objective

The use of online adaptive radiotherapy (oART) is increasing to treat tumours in dynamic regions of the patient. Dynamic regions are referred to as regions where anatomy changes considerably per delivered fraction (e.g. organ filling or weight loss). Examples of these regions are pelvic cancers, due to the constant change in bladder size and shape, pushing the tumor in different positions. With this new treatment approach, new treatment systems have been developed (e.g. Varian Ethos). To simulate the different stuations in bladder size and shape, this work aims to develop a first tissue-equivalent anthropomorphic phantom including a dynamic bladder.
